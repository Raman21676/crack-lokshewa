The Staff Nurse position under Lok Sewa Aayog (Public Service Commission) Nepal is one of the most important healthcare roles in the country's public health system. Staff Nurses serve in government hospitals, health posts, primary health centers, and community health units across all seven provinces of Nepal. With Nepal's commitment to achieving Universal Health Coverage and strengthening its public health infrastructure, the demand for qualified Staff Nurses continues to grow. This guide provides a complete roadmap for aspiring candidates preparing for this competitive examination.

Staff Nurse Exam Overview

The Staff Nurse post is classified as a non-gazetted 4th level (श्रेणी) position in the health service group. Selected candidates are posted to various government health facilities where they provide direct patient care, assist in clinical procedures, administer medications, maintain patient records, and participate in community health programs. The position offers a stable career in Nepal's public health sector with opportunities for promotion to Nursing Officer and higher administrative roles.

Position Details at a Glance

  • Post Level: Non-Gazetted 4th Level (कर्मचारी श्रेणी)
  • Service Group: Health Service
  • Examining Body: Lok Sewa Aayog (Public Service Commission Nepal)
  • Examination Phases: Preliminary (Objective), Main (Written), and Interview
  • Preliminary Questions: 50 MCQs in 45 minutes with negative marking
  • Full Marks (Preliminary): 100 marks (2 marks per correct answer, -0.5 for incorrect)

Eligibility Criteria

To be eligible for the Staff Nurse (4th level) vacancy, candidates must satisfy the following requirements:

  • Educational Qualification: Proficiency Certificate Level (PCL) in Nursing (Staff Nurse) from a recognized nursing institution affiliated with the Nepal Nursing Council and the Council for Technical Education and Vocational Training (CTEVT).
  • Registration: Must be registered with the Nepal Nursing Council and hold a valid nursing license.
  • Age Limit: Generally 18 to 35 years for general candidates. The upper age limit is extended to 40 years for female candidates and candidates from marginalized communities as per current government provisions.
  • Citizenship: Nepali citizenship is mandatory.
  • Character: Good moral character with no criminal conviction involving moral turpitude.
  • Health: Physically and mentally fit to perform nursing duties.

Exam Pattern and Structure

The Staff Nurse selection process follows the standard three-phase examination system. Understanding the format and weightage of each phase is essential for effective preparation.

Phase 1: Preliminary Examination

The preliminary exam is a competitive screening test with 50 multiple-choice questions to be answered in 45 minutes. The exam uses negative marking — each correct answer awards 2 marks, while each incorrect answer deducts 0.5 marks. The subject distribution is approximately:

Subject AreaApproximate WeightageKey Topics
Nursing Science & Clinical Subjects40-50%Anatomy, Physiology, Fundamentals, Medical-Surgical, Pediatric, Mental Health Nursing
Community Health Nursing15-20%Primary Health Care, Immunization, Family Planning, Disease Surveillance
General Knowledge & Current Affairs15-20%Nepal GK, Health Policies, Current Events
Constitution & Public Health Act10-15%Constitution 2072, Health-related laws
Mathematics, IQ & English5-10%Basic Math, Logical Reasoning, Grammar

Phase 2: Main Examination

Candidates who clear the preliminary exam (usually 5-10 times the number of available posts) are invited to the main examination. The main exam consists of:

  • First Paper (Compulsory): Essay writing, précis, comprehension, and grammar in Nepali or English. This tests the candidate's communication and analytical abilities.
  • Second Paper (Subject/Service-Related): In-depth questions on nursing theory, clinical procedures, community health, and nursing administration. Questions may be short-answer, long-answer, or case-study based.

Phase 3: Interview

The interview panel includes Lok Sewa commissioners, health service experts, and nursing professionals. Candidates are assessed on their clinical knowledge, ethical reasoning, communication skills, awareness of Nepal's health system, and commitment to public service. The interview typically carries 20 marks.

Detailed Syllabus for Staff Nurse Exam

The Lok Sewa Aayog syllabus for Staff Nurse covers a broad range of nursing and health science subjects. Mastery of each domain is necessary for success in both preliminary and main examinations.

1. Anatomy and Physiology

  • Structure and function of cells, tissues, organs, and organ systems
  • Skeletal system: bones, joints, types of fractures
  • Muscular system: types of muscles, muscle contraction
  • Circulatory system: heart structure, blood vessels, blood composition, cardiac cycle
  • Respiratory system: lungs, breathing mechanism, gas exchange
  • Digestive system: organs, digestion, absorption, metabolism
  • Urinary system: kidneys, nephron, urine formation
  • Nervous system: brain, spinal cord, nerves, reflex arc
  • Endocrine system: glands, hormones, and their functions
  • Reproductive system: male and female anatomy, menstrual cycle
  • Integumentary system: skin structure, wound healing
  • Sensory organs: eye, ear, nose, tongue, skin

2. Fundamentals of Nursing

  • Concepts of health, illness, and wellness
  • Nursing theories and philosophies: Florence Nightingale, Virginia Henderson, Dorothea Orem
  • Nursing process: assessment, diagnosis, planning, implementation, evaluation (ADPIE)
  • Vital signs: temperature, pulse, respiration, blood pressure, pain assessment
  • Asepsis and infection control: hand hygiene, sterilization, disinfection, standard precautions
  • Medication administration: routes, dosage calculation, the five rights, documentation
  • Patient hygiene and comfort: bed bath, oral care, positioning, pressure sore prevention
  • Therapeutic communication and nurse-patient relationship
  • Documentation and record-keeping in nursing
  • Patient safety: fall prevention, restraint use, identification protocols

3. Medical-Surgical Nursing

  • Fluid, electrolyte, and acid-base balance
  • Preoperative and postoperative nursing care
  • Wound care: types of wounds, wound healing phases, dressing techniques
  • Oncology nursing: cancer types, chemotherapy, radiation care, palliative care
  • Cardiovascular disorders: hypertension, coronary artery disease, heart failure, arrhythmias
  • Respiratory disorders: COPD, asthma, pneumonia, tuberculosis
  • Gastrointestinal disorders: peptic ulcer, hepatitis, cirrhosis, appendicitis
  • Neurological disorders: stroke, epilepsy, meningitis, Parkinson's disease
  • Endocrine disorders: diabetes mellitus, thyroid disorders, Cushing's syndrome
  • Orthopedic conditions: fractures, joint replacements, arthritis
  • Emergency and disaster nursing: triage, CPR, first aid, disaster management

4. Pediatric Nursing

  • Growth and development from infancy through adolescence
  • Immunization schedule in Nepal: BCG, DPT, Polio, Measles, Hepatitis B, Japanese Encephalitis, COVID-19
  • Common childhood illnesses: diarrhea, pneumonia, malnutrition, febrile seizures
  • Infant and child feeding: breastfeeding, complementary feeding, nutritional requirements
  • Neonatal care: APGAR scoring, neonatal resuscitation, low birth weight management
  • Child safety and accident prevention

5. Community Health Nursing

  • Concepts of primary health care (PHC) and Alma-Ata declaration
  • Community health assessment and health needs identification
  • Family planning methods: temporary and permanent, counseling
  • Maternal and child health (MCH) programs in Nepal
  • National immunization program and cold chain management
  • Communicable disease control: tuberculosis, HIV/AIDS, malaria, dengue, COVID-19
  • Non-communicable disease prevention: hypertension, diabetes, cancer screening
  • Safe motherhood program: antenatal care, institutional delivery, postnatal care
  • Nutrition programs: vitamin A supplementation, iron/folic acid, iodine
  • Water, sanitation, and hygiene (WASH) promotion
  • Nepal's health system: Ministry of Health, provincial health directorates, local health governance

6. Mental Health (Psychiatric) Nursing

  • Mental health concepts and mental illness classification
  • Common psychiatric disorders: depression, anxiety, schizophrenia, bipolar disorder
  • Therapeutic communication with mentally ill patients
  • Psychopharmacology: antipsychotics, antidepressants, anxiolytics, mood stabilizers
  • Substance abuse and addiction nursing
  • Suicide prevention and crisis intervention
  • Community mental health programs in Nepal

7. Midwifery and Gynecological Nursing

  • Anatomy and physiology of the female reproductive system
  • Normal pregnancy, labor, and puerperium
  • Antenatal care: assessment, screening, counseling
  • Stages of labor and delivery: mechanisms, nursing care
  • Postnatal care: breastfeeding support, complication detection, family planning counseling
  • Common obstetric complications: hemorrhage, pre-eclampsia, eclampsia, obstructed labor
  • Newborn care: immediate care, thermal protection, feeding, screening
  • Common gynecological conditions and their management

Preparation Strategy

A well-organized study plan is critical given the extensive syllabus. Here is a recommended 4-month preparation strategy:

Recommended 4-Month Study Plan

Month 1: Review Anatomy, Physiology, and Fundamentals of Nursing thoroughly. These form the foundation for all clinical subjects.

Month 2: Cover Medical-Surgical Nursing and Pediatric Nursing. Focus on disease pathology, signs and symptoms, nursing interventions, and medications.

Month 3: Study Community Health Nursing, Mental Health Nursing, and Midwifery. Pay special attention to Nepal's national health programs.

Month 4: Solve previous year questions, take daily mock tests, revise weak topics, and practice descriptive answers for the main exam.

Daily Study Routine

  • Morning (3 hours): Read theory from nursing textbooks — focus on one major subject per week.
  • Afternoon (2 hours): Solve MCQs related to the morning topic. Maintain a mistake notebook.
  • Evening (1-2 hours): Review Nepal's health programs, current health news, and GK. Take a timed mock test every alternate day.

  • Stages of wound healing and pressure sore grading
  • APGAR scoring components and interpretation
  • Normal labor stages and nursing care at each stage
  • Signs and symptoms of common communicable diseases in Nepal
  • Family planning methods and their effectiveness
  • Emergency management: CPR steps, choking, hemorrhage control

Nepal's Key Health Programs to Remember

Questions about Nepal's national health programs frequently appear in the Staff Nurse exam. Ensure you know the following:

  • Safe Motherhood Program: Aims to reduce maternal and neonatal mortality through antenatal care, skilled birth attendance, and emergency obstetric care
  • National Immunization Program: Provides free vaccines to all children — BCG, Penta (DPT-HepB-Hib), Polio, PCV, IPV, Measles-Rubella, Japanese Encephalitis
  • National Tuberculosis Program: DOTS strategy for TB detection and treatment
  • National HIV/AIDS and STI Control Program: Prevention, testing, and ART services
  • Family Planning Program: Provision of contraceptives, counseling, and sterilization services
  • Nutrition Program: Vitamin A supplementation, iron/folic acid distribution, growth monitoring
  • Community-Based Integrated Management of Childhood Illness (CB-IMCI): Community-level management of common childhood illnesses
  • Ayurveda and Alternative Medicine Program: Integration of traditional medicine into primary healthcare

Career Scope and Salary

A Staff Nurse appointed through Lok Sewa Aayog starts at the 4th level with a basic salary of approximately NPR 26,000+. With allowances, the gross monthly salary ranges from NPR 35,000 to 45,000 initially. Promotion opportunities exist to Senior Staff Nurse, Nursing Officer (अस्पताले), and eventually to administrative positions like Nursing Superintendent. Government nurses also enjoy pension benefits, health insurance, paid leave, and opportunities for specialized training both within Nepal and abroad.

Common Preparation Mistakes

  • Ignoring community health nursing — it carries significant weightage
  • Not memorizing Nepal's national health programs and immunization schedule
  • Focusing only on clinical subjects while neglecting GK and constitution
  • Not practicing descriptive answers for the main examination
  • Skipping previous year question paper analysis
