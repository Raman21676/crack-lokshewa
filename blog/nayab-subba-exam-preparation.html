The Nayab Subba examination is a crucial milestone for civil service aspirants in Nepal. Conducted by the Lok Sewa Aayog (Public Service Commission) for non-gazetted 6th level (KHA) positions, this exam opens doors to mid-level administrative roles across federal ministries, provincial governments, and local level offices. Whether you are a Kharidar seeking promotion or a fresh candidate applying through open competition, understanding the exam pattern and having a solid preparation strategy is essential.

This guide covers everything you need to know about the Nayab Subba exam — from eligibility and syllabus to daily study plans and free practice tests. Let us dive in.

What is the Nayab Subba Exam?

Nayab Subba (Deputy Subba) is a non-gazetted 6th level post in Nepal's civil service hierarchy. It sits above Kharidar (5th level) and below Adhikrit/Subba (7th level). Nayab Subbas are responsible for mid-level administrative work, file processing, coordination between departments, and assisting higher-level officers in policy implementation.

The exam is conducted in two stages: the Preliminary Examination (objective/MCQ type) and the Main Examination (subjective/descriptive type + interview). Only candidates who secure the cutoff marks in the preliminary exam are invited to the main examination.

Nayab Subba Salary Scale (2081/82)

The starting salary for a Nayab Subba is approximately NPR 31,802 per month (as per the new consolidated pay scale), along with allowances, provident fund, pension benefits, and other government perks.

Eligibility Criteria for Nayab Subba

The eligibility criteria differ slightly depending on whether you are applying through open competition or internal promotion:

For Open Competition:

  • Nationality: Nepali citizen
  • Age: 18 to 35 years (relaxation up to 40 years for female and reserved category candidates)
  • Education: Bachelor's degree or equivalent from a recognized university
  • Character: Good moral character, no criminal conviction

For Internal Promotion (Kharidar to Nayab Subba):

  • Service Period: Minimum 5 years of continuous service as Kharidar
  • Age: As per PSC rules for promotion candidates
  • Performance: Satisfactory performance reports during service

Always verify the specific requirements in the official vacancy notice, as they may vary by service group and post.

Detailed Nayab Subba Syllabus

The Nayab Subba syllabus is more comprehensive than the Kharidar syllabus. Here is the subject-wise breakdown:

1. General Knowledge (GK) — 25-30 Marks

  • Nepal History: Ancient, medieval, modern, and contemporary history; political movements; unification to federalism
  • Geography: Physical, economic, and human geography of Nepal; provinces and local structures
  • Constitution of Nepal 2072: Fundamental rights, directive principles, federal structure, constitutional bodies, election systems
  • Current Affairs: National and international events, government policies, budget highlights, bilateral relations
  • Science & Technology: Everyday science, environmental science, ICT developments
  • Economy: Economic survey data, GDP, inflation, trade balance, remittance, development plans

2. Service-Related Knowledge — 20-25 Marks

  • Civil Service Act 2049 and Civil Service Regulations 2050
  • Employee Adjustment Act 2074
  • Public Service Commission related laws and procedures
  • Local Government Operation Act 2074 and inter-governmental coordination
  • Financial procedures, public procurement, and audit mechanisms
  • Office management, record keeping, and administrative procedures

3. Governance & Society (G&S) — 15-20 Marks

This is a unique section for Nayab Subba that tests understanding of governance:

  • Concept of good governance, transparency, and accountability
  • E-governance and digital Nepal framework
  • Social inclusion, gender equality, and minority rights
  • Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) and Nepal's progress
  • Human rights conventions and Nepal's commitments
  • Disaster management and climate change policies

4. Mathematics — 10-15 Marks

  • Arithmetic: Percentage, profit/loss, interest, ratio, averages, partnership
  • Algebra: Linear equations, quadratic equations, sets, functions
  • Geometry: Area, volume, coordinate geometry basics
  • Statistics: Mean, median, mode, standard deviation, data interpretation
  • Time-work, pipes-cisterns, and speed-distance problems

5. IQ and Reasoning — 10-15 Marks

  • Number and letter series, coding-decoding, analogies
  • Logical reasoning, syllogisms, statement-conclusion
  • Direction sense, blood relations, calendar problems
  • Non-verbal reasoning and figure-based questions

6. English or Nepali Language — 10 Marks

Depending on the vacancy notice, candidates may be tested on:

  • English: Grammar, vocabulary, comprehension, error detection, sentence improvement
  • Nepali: Byakaran (grammar), samas, sandhi, upsarga, pratyaya, and comprehension

Nayab Subba Preparation Strategy

A strategic approach is necessary because the Nayab Subba syllabus is vast. Here is a proven preparation strategy:

Phase 1: Foundation Building (Months 1-3)

Focus on completing the syllabus thoroughly. Read standard books for GK, Constitution, and Service-Related subjects. Make short notes for quick revision. Do not rush — understanding concepts is more important than memorization at this stage.

Phase 2: Mathematics, IQ & Language (Month 4)

Dedicate this month to scoring subjects. Practice math formulas daily, solve 50+ IQ questions, and work on English/Nepali grammar rules. These sections are highly scoring if practiced consistently.

Phase 3: MCQ Practice & Revision (Month 5)

Start solving chapter-wise MCQs from reliable question banks. Focus on previous year questions (PYQs) from the last 10 years. Identify your weak areas and revise them intensely.

Phase 4: Mock Tests & Time Management (Month 6)

Take one full-length mock test daily under timed conditions. Analyze your performance after each test. Work on improving speed and accuracy. In the final 2 weeks, increase to 2-3 mocks per day.

Main Examination and Interview Preparation

Once you clear the preliminary exam, the real challenge begins. The Nayab Subba main examination typically includes:

  • First Paper: Compulsory Nepali or English (essay, précis, comprehension, grammar)
  • Second Paper: Compulsory subject paper (GK, Constitution, Service-Related)
  • Third Paper: Optional subject or service group specific paper

For the interview, focus on:

  • Your biodata and academic background
  • Current national issues and your opinion on them
  • Knowledge of the office/department you are applying to
  • Communication skills and confidence
  • Dress formally, be punctual, and maintain eye contact

Key Success Tips for Nayab Subba Aspirants

  1. Read the Constitution Thoroughly: Articles 1-100, fundamental rights, and constitutional bodies are frequently asked.
  2. Stay Updated on Governance: Read about federalism, inter-governmental fiscal transfer, and local governance innovations.
  3. Practice Writing: For the main exam, practice writing essays and answers in Nepali or English daily.
  4. Join Study Groups: Discussing with peers helps clarify doubts and keeps motivation high.
  5. Use Technology: Use apps and online platforms like Crack Lokshewa for daily practice.
  6. Take Care of Health: A healthy mind resides in a healthy body. Sleep 6-7 hours and exercise regularly.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the Nayab Subba exam in Nepal?
The Nayab Subba exam is a non-gazetted 6th level (KHA) examination conducted by Lok Sewa Aayog for mid-level administrative positions in Nepal's civil service. It is a promotion and direct recruitment exam for positions like Nayab Subba in various government offices.
What is the syllabus of Nayab Subba exam?
The Nayab Subba syllabus includes General Knowledge, Service-Related Knowledge, Governance & Society (G&S), Mathematics, IQ/Reasoning, and English or Nepali language (depending on the post). The preliminary exam has 100 MCQs with negative marking.
How many months are needed to prepare for Nayab Subba?
Most successful candidates prepare for 6 to 9 months for the Nayab Subba exam. This includes 3-4 months for syllabus completion, 2 months for revision and MCQ practice, and 1-2 months for intensive mock testing. Consistent daily study of 5-6 hours is recommended.
What is the negative marking in Nayab Subba exam?
Yes, the Nayab Subba preliminary exam follows the standard Lok Sewa Aayog negative marking system. For each wrong answer, 0.25 marks are deducted. Each correct answer carries 1 mark, and the total exam is 100 marks with 100 MCQs in 45 minutes.
Can Kharidars apply for Nayab Subba through promotion?
Yes, serving Kharidars can apply for Nayab Subba through internal promotion after completing the required service period (typically 5 years) and meeting other eligibility criteria set by Lok Sewa Aayog. Both open competition and promotion tracks are available.
