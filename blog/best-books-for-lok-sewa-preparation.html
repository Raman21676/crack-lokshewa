How to Choose the Right Books for Lok Sewa

Before diving into specific recommendations, it is important to understand what makes a book truly useful for Lok Sewa preparation:

  • Aligned with Syllabus: The book must cover the official Lok Sewa Aayog syllabus for your target post.
  • Updated Content: GK, current affairs, and even constitutional provisions change over time. Always prefer the latest editions.
  • Previous Year Questions: Books that include solved previous year questions help you understand the exam pattern and frequently asked topics.
  • Practice Sets: Model sets and practice questions are essential for building speed and accuracy.
  • Clear Language: Books should explain concepts in simple Nepali or English, depending on your comfort.

Best General Knowledge (GK) Books

General Knowledge forms the largest portion of the preliminary exam for all Lok Sewa posts. A strong GK foundation requires knowledge of Nepali history, geography, politics, culture, science, and international affairs.

Nepali Language GK Books

  • Lok Sewa Aayog Samanya Gyan by Jagadamba Publications - One of the most popular GK books in Nepal. Covers Nepal's history, geography, constitution, science, and current affairs in Nepali. Updated annually.
  • Samanya Gyan Sangraha by Samiksha Publication - Comprehensive GK coverage with practice MCQs at the end of each chapter. Excellent for Kharidar and Nayab Subba level.
  • Nepal Ko Bhugol Ra Itihas by Pramod Pradhan - Deep dive into Nepal's geography and history, essential for GK depth.
  • Samsamyik Gyan by Asmita Publication - Best for current affairs section. Updated every few months with the latest national and international events.
  • GK Digest Nepal by Kitab Nepal - Condensed GK notes organized by topic, ideal for quick revision before exams.

English Language GK Books

  • General Knowledge Manual by Edgar Thorpe and Showick Thorpe - A standard reference for world GK, science, and Indian subcontinent affairs. Useful for Adhikrit-level exams.
  • Lucent's General Knowledge - Widely used in South Asia. Covers history, geography, Indian polity, economy, science, and miscellaneous topics in English.
  • Manorama Yearbook - Annual publication with comprehensive current affairs, useful for the general awareness section.

Best Constitution of Nepal Books

The Constitution of Nepal 2072 (2015) is a critical subject for all Lok Sewa exams. Questions test knowledge of fundamental rights, directive principles, governance structure, federalism, and local government provisions.

  • Nepal Ko Sambidhan 2072 by Bidhya Raj Poudel (Jagadamba) - The most recommended Nepali-language constitution book with article-wise explanation, MCQs, and comparison with previous constitutions.
  • Constitution of Nepal: Simplified by Samiksha Publication - Easy-to-understand explanation in Nepali with tables, charts, and practice questions.
  • Nepal Ko Sambidhan Ek Parichaya by Bipin Adhikari - Academic-level analysis of the constitution, ideal for Adhikrit and Sakha Adhikrit candidates who need deeper understanding.
  • Sambidhan Sawal Jwaf by Asmita - Question-answer format focusing on frequently asked constitution MCQs.
  • Official Constitution Text (Nepali/English) - Published by the Law, Justice and Parliamentary Affairs Ministry. Every serious candidate should read the original text at least once.

Best IQ and Reasoning Books

IQ (Intelligence Quotient) and reasoning tests assess logical thinking, pattern recognition, problem-solving, and mental agility. This section is often the tie-breaker in competitive exams.

  • Buddhi Parikshan Tatha Tarkashakti by Jagadamba Publications - The gold standard for IQ preparation in Nepali. Covers verbal reasoning, non-verbal reasoning, analogy, series, coding-decoding, and puzzles.
  • IQ and Reasoning by Samiksha Publication - Comprehensive coverage with hundreds of practice questions organized by topic.
  • IQ Test and Mental Ability by Ganga Ram Sharma - Good for beginners with step-by-step explanations.
  • A Modern Approach to Verbal and Non-Verbal Reasoning by R.S. Aggarwal - English-language classic used across South Asian competitive exams. Excellent for Adhikrit-level reasoning.
  • Analytical Reasoning by M.K. Pandey - Best for logical and analytical reasoning sections in English.

Best Mathematics Books

Mathematics questions in Lok Sewa exams focus on arithmetic, algebra, geometry, and basic statistics. The difficulty level varies by post - Kharidar level is simpler, while Adhikrit level includes more complex problems.

  • Ganit Tatha Samanya Jagir Tayari by Jagadamba - Nepali-language math book tailored for Lok Sewa with shortcut methods and solved examples.
  • Complete Mathematics by Samiksha Publication - Topic-wise coverage with practice sets. Includes arithmetic, algebra, geometry, and statistics.
  • Quantitative Aptitude by R.S. Aggarwal - The most trusted English math book for competitive exams in South Asia. Covers all topics with thousands of solved examples.
  • Fast Track Objective Arithmetic by Rajesh Verma - Focuses on shortcut tricks and time-saving techniques for solving math problems quickly.
  • Magical Book on Quicker Maths by M. Tyra - Advanced shortcut methods for candidates targeting officer-level exams.

Best English Language Books

English is tested in all Lok Sewa exams, with higher weightage in Adhikrit and Sakha Adhikrit posts. Questions cover grammar, vocabulary, comprehension, precis, and essay writing.

  • Objective General English by S.P. Bakshi - Comprehensive coverage of grammar, vocabulary, comprehension, and sentence arrangement. Ideal for all levels.
  • High School English Grammar and Composition by Wren and Martin - The classic reference for English grammar rules. Every serious candidate should own this.
  • Word Power Made Easy by Norman Lewis - Best book for vocabulary building through root words and etymology.
  • English for General Competitions by Neetu Singh - Practice-oriented book with previous year questions from various competitive exams.
  • Lok Sewa Aayog Angreji by Jagadamba - Nepali-English bilingual book specifically designed for Lok Sewa English preparation.

Best Nepali Language Books

Nepali language is compulsory for all Lok Sewa exams. The syllabus includes grammar (byakaran), essay writing, precis writing, comprehension, and translation.

  • Nepali Byakaran by Madhav Prasad Ghimire - The most authoritative Nepali grammar reference book. Covers sandhi, samas, karak, vachya, and alankar.
  • Nepali Rachana Tatha Byakaran by Jagadamba - Combines grammar rules with essay and letter writing practice for Lok Sewa.
  • Lok Sewa Aayog Nepali by Samiksha Publication - Targeted Nepali preparation with model essays, precis, and grammar MCQs.
  • Nepali Bhasha Pravesika by Chudamani Bandhu - Academic-level Nepali language book for deep grammatical understanding.
  • Nepali Nibandha Tatha Patra Lekhan by various authors - Practice books for essay and letter writing.

Service-Related Subject Books

For the main examination and some preliminary exams, candidates need subject-specific books based on their service group.

For Administration and General Service

  • Public Administration by Laxmi Prasad Baral - Covers theories, principles, and Nepal's administrative structure.
  • Nepal Ko Prashasan by Dambar Bir Karki - Nepali-language public administration book.
  • Management Principles and Practices by P. Subba Rao - For management-related service groups.

For Education Service

  • Shikshashastra by Jagadamba - Educational philosophy, psychology, and Nepal's education system.
  • Foundations of Education by S.K. Kochhar - English reference for educational theories.

For Health Service

  • Community Health Nursing by K.P. Neupane - Covers Nepal's primary health care system and community health programs.
  • Medical-Surgical Nursing by Brunner and Suddarth - Standard English reference for nursing professionals.

For Engineering and Technical Services

  • Civil Engineering Subjectives by R. Agor - For civil engineering service preparation.
  • Computer Science and IT by various publishers - Subject-specific technical books for IT Officer and Computer Operator posts.

How to Use Books Effectively

Owning the right books is only half the battle. Here is how to extract maximum value from your study materials:

1. Active Reading

Do not just read passively. Underline key facts, write margin notes, and create summary sheets for each chapter. Active engagement improves retention by up to 70% compared to passive reading.

2. Make Your Own Notes

After reading a chapter, close the book and write down what you remember. This forces your brain to process and organize information. Your notes will become your primary revision tool in the final weeks.

3. Solve Practice Questions Immediately

After finishing a topic, solve all related MCQs before moving to the next chapter. This reinforces learning and highlights weak areas that need revisiting.

4. Use the Pomodoro Technique

Study in focused 25-minute blocks followed by 5-minute breaks. After 4 blocks, take a longer 15-30 minute break. This maintains concentration and prevents burnout during long study sessions.

5. Revise in Cycles

Plan at least 3 complete revisions of every book. First reading builds understanding, second reading reinforces memory, and third reading ensures recall under exam pressure.

6. Combine Books with Mock Tests

Books provide knowledge; mock tests build exam skills. After completing a subject from books, take timed mock tests on Crack Lokshewa to assess your readiness.

Common Mistakes in Book-Based Preparation

  • Buying too many books and not finishing any of them thoroughly
  • Using outdated editions with old constitutional provisions or obsolete current affairs
  • Reading without solving practice questions
  • Not making personal notes and relying solely on the book text
  • Ignoring the official syllabus and reading irrelevant topics
  • Spending too much time on one subject while neglecting others

Frequently Asked Questions

Which is the best GK book for Lok Sewa Aayog preparation?
Lok Sewa Aayog Samanya Gyan by Jagadamba Publications is the most popular and trusted GK book for Lok Sewa preparation in Nepal. For English-medium preparation, Lucent's General Knowledge is widely recommended.
How many books should I study for Lok Sewa preparation?
For each subject, 2-3 quality books are sufficient. In total, 8-12 well-chosen books across all subjects (GK, Constitution, Math, IQ, English, Nepali) should be enough if studied thoroughly with proper notes and revision.
Are old edition books useful for Lok Sewa?
Old editions can be used for static subjects like math, IQ, and basic grammar. However, for GK, current affairs, and constitution, always use the latest edition as facts, laws, and political situations change frequently.
Can I clear Lok Sewa without buying books?
Yes, it is possible using free online resources, previous year question papers, and platforms like Crack Lokshewa. However, books provide structured, comprehensive coverage that is difficult to match with fragmented online content alone.
