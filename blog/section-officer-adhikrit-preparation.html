The Section Officer (Adhikrit) examination is the gateway to becoming a gazetted officer in Nepal's civil service. Conducted by the Lok Sewa Aayog (Public Service Commission) for 7th and 8th level positions, this exam is considered one of the most prestigious and competitive in the country. Thousands of candidates dream of wearing the gazetted officer badge, but only a handful make it through the rigorous selection process.

If you are aiming for the Adhikrit exam in 2082, this guide is your complete companion. We will cover the exam structure, detailed syllabus, preparation strategies for every subject, interview tips, and free mock tests to help you succeed.

What is the Section Officer (Adhikrit) Exam?

The term "Adhikrit" refers to a gazetted officer in Nepal's administrative service hierarchy. Section Officers are appointed at the 7th or 8th level and are responsible for managing sections within government ministries, departments, and constitutional bodies. Their duties include policy drafting, file processing, inter-departmental coordination, supervising junior staff, and assisting under-secretaries and joint secretaries in decision-making.

The Adhikrit exam is conducted in three stages: Preliminary Examination (objective), Main Examination (subjective/descriptive), and Interview. Each stage filters out a significant number of candidates, making the final selection highly competitive.

Adhikrit Salary & Perks

A newly appointed Section Officer earns approximately NPR 43,689 per month (consolidated pay scale). Additional benefits include Dearness Allowance, House Rent Allowance, medical benefits, provident fund, pension, Dashain allowance, and opportunities for foreign training and study leave.

Eligibility Criteria for Section Officer

To apply for the Adhikrit examination, candidates must meet the following criteria:

  • Nationality: Nepali citizen
  • Age Limit: 21 to 40 years for open competition (relaxation for female, disabled, and marginalized groups as per rules)
  • Educational Qualification: Bachelor's degree or equivalent from a recognized university. Some technical posts may require specific degrees.
  • Experience: For some posts, relevant work experience may be required. Check the vacancy notice carefully.
  • Character: Good moral character with no criminal record involving moral turpitude

For promotion candidates (Nayab Subba to Adhikrit), a minimum service period (usually 7 years) and satisfactory performance evaluations are required.

Stage 2: Main Examination

The main examination is descriptive/subjective and carries the real weight in the final merit list:

  • First Paper: Compulsory Nepali (Essay, précis, comprehension, grammar) — 100 marks
  • Second Paper: Compulsory English (Essay, précis, comprehension, grammar) — 100 marks
  • Third Paper: Compulsory subject (General Knowledge, Constitution, Administrative Science) — 100 marks
  • Fourth Paper: Optional subject or service group specific paper — 100 marks

Stage 3: Interview

The interview panel typically awards marks based on:

  • Personality and communication skills
  • Knowledge of current national and international affairs
  • Understanding of administrative procedures and governance
  • Problem-solving ability and decision-making skills
  • Motivation and clarity of purpose

Detailed Adhikrit Syllabus

1. General Knowledge — 25-30 Marks (Preliminary)

  • Nepal History: Comprehensive history from the Gopal dynasty to the federal democratic republic; detailed study of political movements (2007, 2046, 2062/63)
  • Geography: Detailed physical, economic, and social geography; climate zones, biodiversity, natural resources, and regional development
  • Constitution of Nepal 2072: In-depth study of all articles, schedules, fundamental rights, directive principles, federal structure, constitutional bodies (Election Commission, CIAA, Attorney General, etc.)
  • Current Affairs: Last 12 months of national and international events, government policies, budget, bilateral treaties, and global developments
  • Science & Technology: Space missions, medical breakthroughs, environmental science, ICT in governance
  • Economy: Economic survey, monetary policy, fiscal policy, trade, remittance, agriculture, industrial policy
  • International Relations: Nepal's foreign policy, SAARC, BIMSTEC, UN, Nepal-India and Nepal-China relations

2. Service-Related Knowledge — 20-25 Marks

  • Civil Service Act 2049, Regulations 2050, and recent amendments
  • Employee Adjustment Act 2074 and its implementation challenges
  • Public Service Commission Act and examination regulations
  • Local Government Operation Act 2074 and inter-governmental relations
  • Financial procedures, budget formulation, and public audit
  • Public procurement rules and contract management
  • Right to Information Act, Good Governance Act, and anti-corruption mechanisms
  • Office procedures, record management, and digital governance initiatives

3. Governance & Society — 15-20 Marks

  • Theories and practices of good governance
  • E-governance, digital Nepal, and service delivery innovation
  • Social inclusion, affirmative action, and minority rights
  • SDGs, climate action, and sustainable development in Nepal
  • Human rights, child rights, and women's empowerment
  • Decentralization and federalism in practice

4. Mathematics — 10-15 Marks

  • Advanced arithmetic, algebra, and geometry
  • Data interpretation (tables, graphs, charts)
  • Probability and basic statistics
  • Financial mathematics relevant to government budgeting

5. IQ & Logical Reasoning — 10-15 Marks

  • Complex number and letter series
  • Advanced coding-decoding and analogy problems
  • Syllogisms, assumptions, and logical deductions
  • Data sufficiency and critical reasoning

6. English/Nepali Language — 10 Marks

  • English: Advanced grammar, vocabulary, reading comprehension, error spotting, sentence rearrangement
  • Nepali: Advanced byakaran, samas, sandhi, alankar, chhand, and unseen passage

Subject-Wise Preparation Tips

How to Prepare General Knowledge for Adhikrit

GK is the backbone of the Adhikrit preliminary exam. Here is how to master it:

  1. Constitution First: The Constitution of Nepal 2072 is the most frequently asked topic. Make notes article-wise. Focus on Part 1 (Constitutional Provisions), Part 3 (Fundamental Rights), and Part 20 (Constitutional Bodies).
  2. History Timeline: Create a chronological timeline of Nepal's history from ancient to modern periods. Memorize key dates, treaties, and personalities.
  3. Current Affairs Daily: Read Gorkhapatra or online news daily. Maintain a current affairs diary. Focus on the 6 months before the exam.
  4. Map Work: Memorize all 7 provinces, their capitals, districts, and major geographical features.
  5. Economy Data: Remember key figures like GDP growth rate, inflation, trade deficit, and budget allocation for major sectors.

How to Prepare Service-Related Subjects

This section differentiates Adhikrit aspirants from lower-level candidates:

  • Read the Civil Service Act 2049 and Regulations 2050 thoroughly. Focus on appointment, promotion, transfer, and disciplinary provisions.
  • Study the Local Government Operation Act 2074 in detail — powers, functions, and revenue sources of local governments.
  • Understand Public Procurement Act basics, tender processes, and contract types.
  • Learn about Right to Information (RTI) procedures and public accountability mechanisms.

How to Prepare for the Main Examination

The main exam is where most candidates struggle. Here is how to prepare:

  • Essay Writing: Practice writing one essay every day on topics like federalism, corruption, education, health, agriculture, and climate change. Structure your essay with introduction, body (3-4 points), and conclusion.
  • Précis Writing: Read editorials from Kantipur or The Kathmandu Post and write 1/3rd length summaries.
  • Comprehension: Practice reading passages and answering questions within time limits.
  • Grammar: For English, focus on tenses, voice, narration, prepositions, and conjunctions. For Nepali, master sandhi, samas, upsarga, pratyaya, and karak.

Interview Preparation for Adhikrit

The interview carries significant weight in the final selection. Here is how to prepare:

  • Know Your Biodata: Be prepared to answer questions about your education, hometown, hobbies, and work experience.
  • Current Affairs: Read national newspapers daily. Be ready to discuss 5-6 major current issues.
  • Governance Issues: Prepare reasoned opinions on federalism, corruption, service delivery, and administrative reform.
  • Dress Code: Wear formal attire (DAura Suruwal or formal suit). Maintain neat appearance.
  • Body Language: Maintain eye contact, sit upright, speak clearly, and show confidence without arrogance.
  • Mock Interviews: Practice with friends or mentors who have appeared for Adhikrit interviews.

⚠️ Common Interview Mistakes

Avoid giving vague answers. If you do not know something, politely say so rather than bluffing. Do not argue with the interview panel. Stay humble, informed, and positive throughout.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the Adhikrit or Section Officer exam?
The Adhikrit (Section Officer) exam is a gazetted officer-level (7th/8th level) examination conducted by Lok Sewa Aayog Nepal. It is one of the most prestigious civil service exams, recruiting officers who handle section-level administrative responsibilities in federal ministries, constitutional bodies, and provincial governments.
What is the exam pattern for Section Officer preliminary?
The Section Officer preliminary exam consists of 100 MCQs worth 100 marks to be completed in 45 minutes. There is negative marking of 0.25 marks per wrong answer. Only candidates who score above the cutoff are eligible for the main examination, which includes subjective papers and an interview.
How tough is the Adhikrit exam compared to Kharidar and Nayab Subba?
The Adhikrit exam is significantly tougher than Kharidar and Nayab Subba due to its broader and deeper syllabus, higher competition, and the demanding main examination which includes essay writing, précis, and service-specific subjective papers. The interview stage also requires strong communication skills and current affairs knowledge.
What is the salary of a Section Officer in Nepal?
The starting salary of a Section Officer (Adhikrit) in Nepal is approximately NPR 43,689 per month (as per the latest pay scale), excluding allowances. With experience and promotions, the salary increases substantially along with pension, provident fund, and other government benefits.
What subjects should I focus on for Adhikrit preliminary?
For Adhikrit preliminary, focus heavily on General Knowledge (especially Constitution and current affairs), Service-Related Knowledge, Governance & Society, Mathematics, IQ/Reasoning, and English/Nepali language. The GK and Service-Related sections carry the highest weightage.
