Understanding the Lok Sewa Exam System

Before diving into preparation, you must understand how the Lok Sewa Aayog examination system works. The process typically involves three stages:

  1. Preliminary Examination (Objective / MCQ): A screening test with 50 multiple-choice questions, usually conducted online or offline within 45 minutes. Negative marking applies.
  2. Main Examination (Subjective / Written): A detailed written test for those who clear the preliminary. It tests in-depth knowledge of relevant subjects.
  3. Interview: The final stage where candidates are evaluated on personality, communication, and general awareness.

Important

The preliminary exam is the biggest filter — typically 90–95% of candidates are eliminated at this stage alone. Your primary focus should be on cracking the prelims with a strong margin.

The 6-Month Study Plan

Here is a proven 6-month plan that works for Kharidar, Nayab Subba, and Adhikrit levels:

Month 1–2: Foundation Building

  • Collect the official syllabus and past 5 years' question papers.
  • Read one standard book per subject (GK, Constitution, IQ, English/Nepali).
  • Make handwritten notes. Studies show handwritten notes improve retention by 40%.
  • Study 4–5 hours daily with complete focus.

Month 3–4: Intensive Practice

  • Solve at least 50 MCQs daily across all subjects.
  • Start taking weekly full-length mock tests.
  • Analyze every mistake and maintain an error log.
  • Revise notes every weekend.

Month 5: Revision & Weak Area Focus

  • Stop reading new books. Focus on revision.
  • Identify your weak subjects and allocate 60% of time to them.
  • Take 2–3 mock tests per week under exam-like conditions.
  • Practice time management — finish 50 questions in 40 minutes.

Month 6: Final Sprint

  • Daily mock tests (one full set every day).
  • Light revision of formulas, dates, and article numbers.
  • Stay calm, eat well, and sleep 7–8 hours.

Success Tip

Candidates who take 20+ full-length mock tests before the real exam have a 3x higher pass rate than those who take fewer than 5. Mock tests build stamina, speed, and confidence.

Subject-Wise Strategy

General Knowledge (GK)

GK is the highest-scoring section. Focus on Nepal's geography, history, polity, economy, international organizations, science, and sports. Read a reliable GK book like "Nepal Ko Samanya Gyan" and supplement with daily current affairs.

Constitution of Nepal

Memorize the preamble, key articles (especially 16–46), schedules, and the three-tier government structure. Constitution questions are highly predictable and scoring.

IQ and Mental Ability

Practice daily. Focus on analogy, series, coding-decoding, and direction sense. IQ skills improve quickly with repetition.

English / Nepali (Language)

For English, focus on grammar, vocabulary, synonyms, antonyms, and comprehension. For Nepali, master grammar rules (Byakaran), sandhi, samas, and chhandalankar.

Subject-Specific (Service-Related)

For posts like Computer Operator, IT Officer, or Staff Nurse, technical subject knowledge carries significant weight. Study your domain subjects deeply.

Daily Schedule for Working Aspirants

If you are working or studying, here is a realistic daily schedule:

  • 5:00 AM – 7:00 AM: Study (new topics, note-making)
  • 7:00 AM – 8:00 AM: Current affairs reading
  • Evening (1 hour): Solve 30–50 MCQs
  • Before bed (30 min): Quick revision of day's notes

This gives you roughly 3.5 to 4 hours daily — enough to crack the exam in 6 months.

Revision Techniques That Actually Work

  1. Spaced Repetition: Revise a topic after 1 day, then 3 days, then 7 days, then 14 days.
  2. Active Recall: Close your book and write down everything you remember before checking.
  3. Teach Someone: Explaining a concept to a friend is one of the best ways to reinforce learning.
  4. Mind Maps: Create visual mind maps for complex topics like governance structure.

Why Mock Tests Are Non-Negotiable

Mock tests serve multiple purposes:

  • Speed Building: You learn to solve 50 questions in 45 minutes.
  • Accuracy Training: You identify whether you are making silly mistakes.
  • Pressure Handling: Simulating exam conditions reduces anxiety on the actual day.
  • Gap Analysis: You discover which subjects need more attention.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Watch Out For These

  • Reading too many books: One good book per subject is better than five average ones.
  • Ignoring negative marking: Do not guess blindly. Eliminate wrong options first.
  • Skipping mock tests: Reading without testing is like driving without a license.
  • Inconsistent study: Studying 10 hours one day and zero the next is worse than 3 hours daily.
  • Neglecting health: Poor sleep and diet destroy memory and focus.

Mental Preparation and Stress Management

The Lok Sewa journey is mentally demanding. Here is how to stay strong:

  1. Set small goals: "Today I will master 20 constitution articles" is better than "I need to pass Lok Sewa."
  2. Exercise daily: Even 20 minutes of walking improves brain function.
  3. Limit social media: Uninstall distracting apps during preparation months.
  4. Join a study group: Peer support keeps motivation high.
  5. Meditate: 10 minutes of daily meditation improves concentration significantly.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many months are needed to prepare for Lok Sewa?
For most candidates, 4 to 6 months of consistent daily study (3–5 hours) is sufficient to clear the preliminary exam. Main exam and interview require additional 2–3 months.

Yes. Many successful candidates prepared while working. The key is a disciplined daily schedule of 3–4 hours and maximizing weekends for mock tests.
