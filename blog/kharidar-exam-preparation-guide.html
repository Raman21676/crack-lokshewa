What is the Kharidar Exam?

The Kharidar exam is the entry point into Nepal's civil service for administrative and clerical roles. Successful candidates are appointed as Kharidars in various government ministries, departments, constitutional bodies, and local level offices. The position offers job security, a decent salary scale (starting at NPR 26,082 under the new pay scale), and opportunities for career progression through internal promotions.

Did You Know?

The Kharidar exam is conducted in two stages: Preliminary (Objective) and Main (Subjective/Interview). Only candidates who pass the preliminary exam with the required cut-off marks are eligible to appear for the main examination.

Eligibility Criteria for Kharidar 2082

Before starting your preparation, ensure you meet the eligibility requirements set by Lok Sewa Aayog:

  • Nationality: Must be a Nepali citizen.
  • Age Limit: Generally 18 to 35 years for open competition. Age relaxation applies for female candidates (up to 40 years) and candidates from certain marginalized groups.
  • Educational Qualification: Minimum completion of SLC/SEE or equivalent. Some positions may require +2 or bachelor's level depending on the specific vacancy notice.
  • Character: Must be of good moral character and not convicted of any criminal offense involving moral turpitude.

Always refer to the latest vacancy notice published by Lok Sewa Aayog, as eligibility criteria may vary slightly depending on the specific post and service group.

Kharidar Syllabus Breakdown

The Kharidar syllabus is divided into four major sections. Below is the detailed subject-wise breakdown with approximate weightage:

1. General Knowledge (GK) — 30-35 Marks

This is the most important and scoring section of the Kharidar exam. Topics include:

  • Nepal History: Ancient, medieval, and modern history of Nepal; unification campaign by Prithvi Narayan Shah; Rana regime; democratic movements of 2007, 2046, and 2062/63.
  • Geography of Nepal: Physical features, climate, rivers, natural resources, provinces, districts, and their capitals.
  • Constitution of Nepal 2072: Preamble, fundamental rights, directive principles, federal structure, three-tier government, constitutional bodies, and key amendments.
  • Current Affairs: National and international events, government policies, budgets, appointments, and important days.
  • Science & Technology: Basic physics, chemistry, biology, and recent technological developments.
  • Economy: Nepal's economic indicators, GDP, remittance, trade, agriculture, and fiscal/monetary policies.

2. Service-Related Knowledge — 25-30 Marks

This section tests your understanding of Nepal's administrative system and civil service regulations:

  • Civil Service Act 2049 and Civil Service Regulations 2050
  • Employee Adjustment Act and federal restructuring
  • Local Government Operation Act 2074
  • Right to Information Act, Good Governance Act
  • Public Procurement Act and Financial Procedures
  • Nepal's administrative structure: federal, provincial, and local levels

3. Mathematics — 15-20 Marks

The math section is based on the Class 10 (SEE) level syllabus:

  • Arithmetic: Percentage, profit/loss, simple & compound interest, ratio & proportion, averages
  • Algebra: Linear equations, quadratic equations, sets, and basic operations
  • Geometry: Area, volume, perimeter, angles, and triangle properties
  • Statistics: Mean, median, mode, and basic data interpretation
  • Time, work, and distance problems

4. IQ and Reasoning — 15-20 Marks

This section tests your logical thinking and problem-solving abilities:

  • Number series and patterns
  • Alphabetical series and coding-decoding
  • Analogies and classifications
  • Direction sense and blood relations
  • Logical Venn diagrams and syllogisms
  • Figure counting and odd-one-out

Mock Test Strategy for Kharidar

Taking mock tests is not just about practice — it is about building exam temperament. Here is how to use mock tests effectively:

  1. Simulate Exam Conditions: Take mocks at the same time as your actual exam slot. Use a timer strictly for 45 minutes.
  2. Analyze Every Mock: Spend at least 30 minutes analyzing your mistakes. Note down weak topics and revise them before the next mock.
  3. Work on Speed: You have only 27 seconds per question. Learn shortcut methods for math and quick elimination techniques for GK.
  4. Strategic Guessing: With 0.25 negative marking, blind guessing is dangerous. Eliminate 2 wrong options first, then make an educated guess.
  5. Track Progress: Maintain a spreadsheet of your mock scores. Aim for consistent improvement rather than random high scores.

Top Tips to Crack the Kharidar Exam

  • Master the Constitution: The Constitution of Nepal 2072 is heavily asked in every Kharidar exam. Make short notes on articles, schedules, and fundamental rights.
  • Focus on Service-Related Laws: Many aspirants ignore this section, but it carries 25-30 marks. Read Civil Service Act, Local Government Operation Act, and Financial Procedures Act carefully.
  • Current Affairs is a Game Changer: Dedicate 1-2 hours daily to current affairs. Focus on the last 6-12 months before the exam date.
  • Math Shortcuts Save Time: Learn Vedic math tricks and shortcut formulas. Practice 20 math problems daily.
  • Don't Neglect IQ: IQ questions are scoring if practiced regularly. Solve at least 50 reasoning questions daily.
  • Revise in Cycles: Use the 3-revision rule — revise a topic after 1 day, 7 days, and 30 days for long-term retention.
  • Stay Updated on Vacancies: Regularly check the Lok Sewa Aayog official website for vacancy notices and syllabus updates.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Most Kharidar aspirants fail not because of lack of effort, but because of these common mistakes:

  1. Ignoring negative marking and attempting all questions blindly.
  2. Reading too many books without revision — "book hopping" without completing any.
  3. Neglecting the service-related section, which is a major scoring area.
  4. Not practicing enough timed mock tests before the exam.
  5. Focusing only on GK and ignoring Math and IQ completely.
  6. Not reading the vacancy notice carefully — missing eligibility or document requirements.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the Kharidar exam in Nepal?
The Kharidar exam is a non-gazetted 5th level (KA) examination conducted by Lok Sewa Aayog (Public Service Commission) of Nepal. It is the entry-level civil service examination for administrative and clerical positions in various government ministries, departments, and local levels.
What is the exam pattern for Kharidar 2082?
The Kharidar preliminary exam consists of 100 multiple-choice questions to be completed in 45 minutes. There is negative marking of 0.25 marks for each wrong answer. The subjects include General Knowledge, Service-Related Knowledge, Mathematics, and IQ/Reasoning.

Is there negative marking in the Kharidar exam?
