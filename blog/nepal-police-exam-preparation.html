Joining the Nepal Police is a dream for thousands of young Nepalis who wish to serve the nation with pride and dedication. The Nepal Police is the primary law enforcement agency of Nepal, responsible for maintaining internal security, preventing crime, and ensuring public safety. Every year, the Nepal Police recruits thousands of personnel across various ranks, including Police Constable (Jawan), Assistant Sub-Inspector (ASI), and Sub-Inspector (SI).

The recruitment process is challenging and multi-staged, involving a written examination, physical fitness test, medical examination, and interview. In this detailed guide, we will walk you through every aspect of Nepal Police exam preparation — from eligibility and syllabus to physical training tips and free mock tests.

Overview of Nepal Police Recruitment

Nepal Police recruitment is conducted through vacancy notices published by the Nepal Police Headquarters or the Public Service Commission (Lok Sewa Aayog), depending on the rank. The process typically involves:

  1. Written Examination: Objective-type MCQ test covering GK, Math, IQ, Language, and Police-related subjects
  2. Physical Fitness Test (PFT): Running, long jump, push-ups, and other physical endurance tests
  3. Medical Examination: Health check-up including vision, hearing, and general fitness
  4. Interview: Personality assessment and verification of documents
  5. Final Selection: Merit list based on combined performance

Nepal Police Ranks (Entry to Senior)

The hierarchy includes: Police Constable (Jawan) → Head Constable (Havaldar) → Assistant Sub-Inspector (ASI) → Sub-Inspector (SI) → Inspector → Deputy Superintendent of Police (DSP) → Superintendent of Police (SP) → Senior Superintendent (SSP) → Deputy Inspector General (DIG) → Inspector General (IGP).

Eligibility Criteria for Nepal Police

Eligibility requirements vary by position. Here are the general criteria:

For Police Constable

  • Nationality: Nepali citizen
  • Age: 18 to 25 years (relaxation for female and reserved categories)
  • Education: SLC/SEE pass or +2 (varies by vacancy)
  • Height (Male): Minimum 5 feet 4 inches (162.56 cm)
  • Height (Female): Minimum 5 feet 2 inches (157.48 cm)
  • Chest (Male): Minimum 31 inches normal, 32 inches expanded
  • Character: Good moral character, no criminal record

For ASI and SI

  • Age: Generally 21 to 30 years (varies by vacancy)
  • Education: Bachelor's degree for SI; +2 or Bachelor's for ASI depending on the notice
  • Experience: For ASI, experience as constable may be required for promotion
  • Physical Standards: Similar or slightly higher than constable requirements

⚠️ Important

Physical standards and eligibility criteria may change with each vacancy notice. Always refer to the official recruitment advertisement published by Nepal Police Headquarters or Lok Sewa Aayog for the most accurate and updated requirements.

Detailed Written Exam Syllabus

1. General Knowledge (GK) — 30-35 Marks

  • Nepal History: Key historical events, unification, democratic movements, constitutional development
  • Geography: Nepal's physical features, provinces, districts, major rivers, mountains, and national parks
  • Constitution of Nepal 2072: Fundamental rights, federal structure, fundamental duties, and local governance
  • Current Affairs: National and international news, government policies, important appointments, awards
  • Science: Basic physics, chemistry, biology, health, and environmental science
  • Economy: Basic economic concepts, Nepal's economic situation, agriculture, and tourism

2. Mathematics — 15-20 Marks

  • Arithmetic: Percentage, profit/loss, interest, ratio, averages
  • Number system, LCM, HCF, and simplification
  • Basic algebra and geometry
  • Time, work, and distance problems
  • Simple data interpretation

3. IQ and Reasoning — 15-20 Marks

  • Number and letter series
  • Coding-decoding and analogies
  • Odd one out and classification
  • Direction sense and blood relations
  • Logical reasoning and pattern recognition

4. Police and Law-Related Knowledge — 15-20 Marks

This section is unique to police recruitment and tests your understanding of:

  • Nepal Police Act and Regulations
  • National Criminal Procedure Code 2074 (Muluki Faujdari Kanun)
  • National Civil Code 2074 (Muluki Dewani Kanun)
  • Traffic rules and regulations
  • Basic concepts of criminal law, evidence, and investigation
  • Human rights in policing and community policing principles
  • Narcotics and drug control laws

5. English or Nepali Language — 10-15 Marks

  • Nepali: Byakaran, samas, sandhi, karak, upsarga, pratyaya, and comprehension
  • English: Grammar, vocabulary, synonyms, antonyms, and basic comprehension

How to Prepare for the Nepal Police Physical Test

Physical fitness cannot be built overnight. Start training at least 2-3 months before the expected test date:

Daily Training Routine

Recommended 8-Week Training Plan

Week 1-2 (Base Building):
Morning: 2-3 km jog, stretching (30 min)
Evening: 50 push-ups, 50 squats, 50 sit-ups (in sets of 10)

Week 3-4 (Endurance):
Morning: 3-4 km run with intervals, long jump practice
Evening: 75 push-ups, 75 squats, plank holds (1 min x 3)

Week 5-6 (Speed):
Morning: 1600m timed run (male) / 800m timed run (female)
Evening: 100 push-ups, pull-up practice, core workout

Week 7-8 (Peak):
Morning: Full PFT simulation every alternate day
Evening: Light recovery workout, stretching, and rest

Nutrition Tips for Police Aspirants

  • Eat a balanced diet rich in protein (eggs, chicken, lentils, dairy)
  • Include complex carbohydrates (rice, roti, oats) for energy
  • Stay hydrated — drink at least 3-4 liters of water daily
  • Avoid junk food, excessive sugar, and alcohol
  • Get 7-8 hours of quality sleep for muscle recovery
  • Consider seasonal fruits and vegetables for vitamins and immunity

Written Exam Preparation Strategy

General Knowledge Preparation

GK forms the largest portion of the written exam. Focus on:

  • Reading Gorkhapatra daily for current affairs
  • Memorizing Nepal's constitutional provisions, especially fundamental rights and duties
  • Studying Nepal's geography — provinces, districts, and their capitals
  • Learning important dates in Nepal's history
  • Keeping track of national and international events 6 months before the exam

Police and Law Preparation

This section separates serious candidates from casual ones:

  • Read the Nepal Police Act and understand the organizational structure
  • Study the Criminal Procedure Code 2074 — arrest procedures, detention, bail, and investigation basics
  • Learn traffic rules, signs, and penalties under the Motor Vehicles and Transport Management Act
  • Understand community policing, human rights in law enforcement, and gender-sensitive policing

Mathematics and IQ

  • Practice 20-30 math problems daily focusing on speed and accuracy
  • Learn shortcut tricks for percentage, ratio, and profit-loss problems
  • Solve 50 IQ questions daily — number series, coding-decoding, and analogies
  • Use previous year question papers to understand the difficulty level

Medical Examination: What to Expect

After clearing the written and physical tests, candidates undergo a medical examination:

  • General Health Check: Blood pressure, pulse rate, temperature
  • Vision Test: 6/6 vision without glasses is preferred; minor corrections may be acceptable
  • Hearing Test: Normal hearing ability in both ears
  • Blood and Urine Tests: Screening for diseases and substance abuse
  • Chest X-Ray: To check for tuberculosis or lung conditions
  • ECG: Heart health assessment
  • Dental Check: Basic oral health

Health Tip

If you have any existing medical conditions, consult a doctor before applying. Address dental issues, vision problems, and chronic conditions well in advance. Maintain a healthy lifestyle throughout your preparation.

Interview Tips for Nepal Police Recruitment

The interview assesses your personality, motivation, and suitability for police service:

  1. Dress Neatly: Wear clean, ironed formal clothes. For males, shirt and pants; for females, kurta-suruwal or formal attire.
  2. Be Punctual: Arrive at least 30 minutes before the scheduled time.
  3. Know Your Documents: Carry all original documents and photocopies neatly arranged.
  4. Stay Confident: Maintain eye contact, speak clearly, and answer honestly.
  5. Show Motivation: Be prepared to explain why you want to join Nepal Police and how you can contribute.
  6. Current Affairs: Be aware of major national issues, especially related to law and order.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Neglecting physical fitness until the last minute — start training early
  • Ignoring the Police Act and law-related sections in the written exam
  • Giving wrong answers due to negative marking — attempt only if reasonably sure
  • Not checking the official vacancy notice carefully — missing important eligibility details
  • Poor time management during the written exam — practice with timed mocks
  • Unhealthy lifestyle before the exam — avoid junk food and late nights

Career Progression in Nepal Police

Joining as a Constable is just the beginning. With dedication, training, and examinations, you can rise through the ranks:

Constable → Head Constable → ASI → SI → Inspector → DSP → SP → SSP → DIG → IGP

Nepal Police also offers specialized training in forensics, cybercrime, counter-terrorism, traffic management, and VIP security. Officers can also pursue higher education with study leave and scholarships.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the Nepal Police recruitment process?
The Nepal Police recruitment process includes a written examination (MCQ type), physical fitness test (PFT), health/medical examination, and a final interview. For Constable positions, the written exam is conducted by the respective Police Recruitment Selection Boards, while ASI and SI exams are often coordinated with Lok Sewa Aayog or internal promotion mechanisms.
What are the physical requirements for Nepal Police Constable?
For male candidates, the minimum height is typically 5 feet 4 inches (162.56 cm) and chest measurement (expanded) is 32 inches. For female candidates, the minimum height is typically 5 feet 2 inches (157.48 cm). Physical tests include running (1600 meters for males, 800 meters for females), long jump, push-ups, and sit-ups within specified time limits. Requirements may vary by vacancy notice.
What is the syllabus for Nepal Police written exam?
The Nepal Police written exam syllabus typically includes General Knowledge (Nepal history, geography, constitution, current affairs), Mathematics (arithmetic, algebra), IQ and Reasoning, English or Nepali language, and Service-Related knowledge (Police Act, Criminal Procedure Code, traffic rules, and general policing concepts).
How can I prepare for Nepal Police physical test?
To prepare for the Nepal Police physical test, start training at least 2-3 months before the exam. Focus on daily running (start with 2-3 km and build up), bodyweight exercises (push-ups, squats, pull-ups), core strengthening, and stretching. Maintain a balanced diet, stay hydrated, and get adequate sleep. Consistency is more important than intensity.
What is the age limit for Nepal Police Constable?
The general age limit for Nepal Police Constable recruitment is 18 to 25 years. However, age relaxation is provided for female candidates, candidates from marginalized communities, and in-service applicants as per the recruitment notice. Always check the latest vacancy announcement for exact age criteria.
