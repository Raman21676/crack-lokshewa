Complete Subject Coverage for Nepal Police Exam

The Nepal Police written examination tests five core competencies. Our mock sets ensure proportional, high-quality coverage of each:

1. General Knowledge & Current Affairs

The GK section forms the backbone of the Nepal Police written test. Our questions span the breadth of knowledge a police officer should possess:

  • Nepal's geography: districts, zones, physical features, climate, and natural resources
  • Historical milestones: unification, democratic movements, republic establishment, and constitutional evolution
  • Political system: federal structure, three tiers of government, election procedures, and key constitutional bodies
  • Economic basics: national budget, major revenue sources, development indicators, and tourism statistics
  • Science and technology: everyday science, health awareness, environmental issues, and digital literacy
  • Sports and culture: national sports, cultural heritage sites, festivals, and internationally recognized Nepali achievements
  • Current affairs 2082: recent appointments, policy changes, international treaties, crime statistics, and national security developments

2. Police Administration, Law & Criminal Justice

This is the differentiator section that generic mock test platforms ignore. Our Nepal Police sets include:

  • Nepal Police Act and organizational structure: hierarchy, ranks, roles, and chain of command
  • Muluki Criminal Procedure Code: arrest procedures, investigation protocols, detention limits, and bail provisions
  • Evidence Act: types of evidence, admissibility, witness protection, and documentary requirements
  • Constitutional provisions related to police: fundamental rights during arrest, habeas corpus, and state obligations
  • Traffic rules and motor vehicle regulations: offenses, penalties, license categories, and road safety protocols
  • Community policing concepts: beat policing, citizen engagement, crime prevention through environmental design
  • Cybercrime basics: IT Act provisions, digital evidence handling, and emerging online threats

3. Mathematics & Numerical Ability

Police officers frequently handle numerical data — crime statistics, budget allocations, personnel ratios. Our math questions include:

  • Basic arithmetic: percentages, averages, ratios, and proportions in policing contexts
  • Profit and loss, simple and compound interest relevant to seized assets and fines
  • Time and distance problems applicable to patrol scheduling and response times
  • Data interpretation from tables and charts: crime rate trends, accident statistics, population demographics
  • Number series, simplification, and approximate calculation under time pressure

4. English Language

English proficiency is increasingly important for higher police posts and international cooperation. Our tests cover:

  • Grammar: tenses, subject-verb agreement, prepositions, articles, and sentence correction
  • Vocabulary: synonyms, antonyms, one-word substitutions, and idiomatic expressions
  • Comprehension: short passages followed by inference-based questions
  • Error detection and sentence improvement
  • Basic translation concepts between Nepali and English

5. IQ, Reasoning & Mental Aptitude

Police work demands quick thinking and pattern recognition. Our reasoning sections include:

  • Verbal analogies and classification problems
  • Number and letter series completion
  • Direction sense and spatial reasoning relevant to patrol navigation
  • Blood relations and age calculation puzzles

How to Use Mock Tests for Maximum Police Exam Success

Passing the Nepal Police written exam requires more than casual quizzing. Follow this systematic approach:

  1. Baseline Diagnostic: Attempt Set 1 under strict exam conditions. No pauses, no help. Your score reveals where you stand after self-study.
  2. Subject Prioritization: If your GK is strong but Law is weak, flip your study schedule. Spend 60% of time on weak areas, 40% maintaining strengths.
  3. Legal Section Mastery: The Police Act and Criminal Procedure Code are high-yield. Read the actual acts alongside mock test practice for deep retention.
  4. Timed Drills: Once familiar with question types, practice individual sections against the clock. Aim for 1 question per minute in GK and Law.
  5. Error Journal: Maintain a notebook of every mistake. Review it twice weekly. Patterns will emerge — perhaps you consistently confuse similar legal sections or misread math word problems.
  6. Progressive Set Advancement: Master Set 1 (consistently 35+/50) before attempting Set 2. Master Set 2 before Set 3. Do not waste hard questions on unprepared attempts.
  7. Physical-Written Balance: Schedule mock tests on days when you are not doing intense physical training. Mental fatigue from running drills will artificially lower your test scores.
  8. Weekly Current Affairs: The GK section rewards fresh knowledge. Dedicate 30 minutes every Sunday to reviewing the week's national news and police-related developments.

Understanding the Nepal Police Selection Process

The journey to wearing the Nepal Police uniform involves multiple rigorous stages. Understanding the full pipeline helps you appreciate where the written exam fits:

Stage 1 — Physical Measurement and Fitness Test: Height, weight, chest measurement (for males), and physical endurance tests including running, long jump, and push-ups. This is the first filter — candidates who fail here are eliminated immediately.

Stage 2 — Written Examination: The intellectual battleground. Typically 100 MCQs covering GK, Law, Math, English, and IQ. Our mock tests prepare you for exactly this challenge. The cutoff varies by post, vacancy, and category but generally requires 50+ marks out of 100 for competitive standing.

Stage 3 — Health Checkup: Comprehensive medical examination including vision, hearing, blood tests, and general fitness verification.

Stage 4 — Interview and Document Verification: Personality assessment, communication skills, motivation evaluation, and verification of educational and character certificates.

Our Nepal Police mock test platform strengthens your performance in Stage 2 — the written exam — which is where many physically strong candidates unexpectedly stumble.

⚠️ Physical + Written = Complete Preparation

Do not neglect either dimension. Many candidates focus exclusively on physical training and treat the written exam as an afterthought. Conversely, book-smart candidates sometimes ignore fitness. Nepal Police selection demands both. Balance your daily schedule between morning physical training and evening academic mock test practice.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What are the different posts in Nepal Police recruitment? +
Nepal Police recruits for multiple positions: Police Constable (Jawan), Assistant Sub-Inspector (ASI), Sub-Inspector (SI), and Inspector. Each post has different educational requirements, physical standards, and examination difficulty levels. Constable is the entry-level position, while Inspector requires a bachelor's degree and passes through Lok Sewa Aayog.
What is the syllabus for Nepal Police written exam 2082? +
The Nepal Police written exam syllabus includes General Knowledge (Nepal and international), Nepali language and grammar, Basic English, Mathematics, and IQ/Reasoning. Higher posts like ASI and SI also include questions on police administration, criminal law, evidence act, and Nepal Police regulations.
How many questions are in the Nepal Police written test? +
The Nepal Police written test typically contains 100 multiple-choice questions to be completed in 45 minutes to 1 hour depending on the post. Our free online mock tests provide 50 questions per set, allowing focused practice while maintaining exam-level intensity.
What are the physical requirements for Nepal Police? +
Physical requirements vary by post and gender. Generally, male candidates must meet minimum height (around 5 feet 4 inches for constable), chest measurement, and running standards. Female candidates have proportionate standards. ASI and Inspector posts may have additional physical and medical criteria. Candidates must clear physical tests before the written examination.
