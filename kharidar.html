Understanding the Kharidar Exam Pattern

The Kharidar examination is conducted by Lok Sewa Aayog and follows a two-stage process: a preliminary (written) exam and a main (written) exam, followed by an interview for shortlisted candidates. The preliminary exam is the most competitive filter — thousands are eliminated at this stage alone.

The preliminary paper contains 100 multiple-choice questions worth 100 marks total, to be completed in 45 minutes. With negative marking of 0.25 per wrong answer, blind guessing can severely hurt your score. The qualifying cutoff varies by vacancy and category (Open, Female, Adivasi/Janajati, Madhesi, Dalit, Disabled, etc.), but a safe score is typically above 55 marks for the open category.

Our kharidar model set 2082 questions are calibrated to this standard. When you consistently score above 28 out of 50 in our medium-difficulty sets, you are on track to clear the real preliminary cutoff.

⚠️ Important: Do Not Neglect the Main Exam

While our mock tests focus on preliminary exam practice, remember that Kharidar selection also depends on the main exam (subjective/Descriptive) and interview. Use these tests to secure your preliminary qualification, then shift to essay writing and interview preparation for the later stages.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is the Kharidar exam in Nepal? +
The Kharidar exam is a non-gazetted 5th level (KA) examination conducted by Lok Sewa Aayog (Public Service Commission) of Nepal. It is the entry-level civil service exam for administrative and clerical positions in government ministries, departments, and local levels. Thousands of candidates appear every year, making competitive practice essential.

The Kharidar syllabus covers four main areas: General Knowledge (Nepal and international), Service-Related Knowledge (public administration, office procedures), Mathematics (basic arithmetic, algebra, geometry), and IQ/Reasoning (verbal, non-verbal, analytical).
